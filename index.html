Exploring Australia's Constitution

What's the "Vibe" of the Constitution?

We often have a "vibe" or a feeling about what a country's most important document should do. For many, it's about fairness and big ideas, like in this classic clip from 'The Castle'.

But how does that vibe match up with the actual words written in Australia's Constitution from 1901? Let's investigate.

1

The "Big Paper" Brainstorm ü§î

Let's start with the big question: What do you think should be in a constitution?

Think of it this way: a constitution is like a rule book for a country. It sets out the most important rules for how the government can act and how people should be treated.

Instructions:

  1. Using the whiteboard below, silently brainstorm your ideas for this "rule book."
  2. Add your ideas using digital sticky notes. Think about: What are the most important rules for treating people fairly? What should the government be allowed (and not allowed) to do? What rights should be protected?
  3. Read your classmates' ideas. You can respond by drawing lines to connect ideas or by adding a new note with a comment or question.
  4. Remember, no talking! All communication happens on the Big Paper.

Vibe vs. Text Showdown ‚öñÔ∏è

It's time to compare. Look back at our "Big Paper" brainstorm from Activity 1 and then think about what you learned from the video in Activity 2.

Guiding Questions for Reflection:

  • What's Missing? Look at the "Big Paper." What ideas did your class have that are not mentioned in the video about the Australian Constitution? (e.g., a bill of rights, mention of a Prime Minister, environmental protection).
  • What's Surprising? What was in the video that you didn't expect? Were you surprised by how much of it is about the mechanics of government (like a "rulebook for politicians")?
  • Why the Difference? Why do you think there's a gap between the "vibe" of what we expect a constitution to be and the actual text of Australia's? Consider when it was written (1901) and what its main purpose was at the time (uniting six British colonies).

Use your answers to these questions to write a concluding paragraph that summarizes the main differences between the 'vibe' you expected and the 'text' you discovered.
